# Employee Onboarding Policy

**Effective Date:** February 1, 2025
**Last Updated:** February 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a standardized onboarding process for all new employees at Acme Corporation. A comprehensive onboarding experience helps new hires become productive team members quickly and increases retention.

## 2. Pre-Arrival (Before Day 1)

### 2.1 HR Responsibilities

- Send offer letter and employment documents within 2 business days of acceptance
- Initiate background check through our third-party provider
- Send welcome email with Day 1 logistics 1 week before start date
- Create accounts in HRIS, email, and Slack
- Order equipment (laptop, monitor, peripherals) at least 2 weeks before start date

### 2.2 Manager Responsibilities

- Assign a buddy/mentor from the team
- Prepare a 30-60-90 day plan
- Schedule key introductory meetings for the first two weeks
- Set up project access and tool permissions
- Send a personal welcome message to the new hire

### 2.3 IT Responsibilities

- Provision laptop with standard software image
- Create email and system accounts
- Set up VPN and MFA credentials
- Prepare access badges

## 3. Day 1

### 3.1 Schedule

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 9:00 AM | Welcome and badge pickup | HR |
| 9:30 AM | HR orientation (benefits, policies, paperwork) | HR |
| 11:00 AM | IT setup and security training | IT |
| 12:00 PM | Team lunch | Manager + Team |
| 1:00 PM | Equipment setup and workspace tour | Buddy |
| 2:00 PM | Manager 1:1 (role expectations, 30-60-90 plan) | Manager |
| 3:00 PM | Self-paced onboarding materials | New Hire |
| 4:00 PM | End of day check-in | Buddy |

### 3.2 Required Paperwork

All paperwork must be completed on Day 1:
- I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification
- W-4 Tax Withholding
- Direct deposit enrollment
- Benefits enrollment (30-day window begins)
- Emergency contact form
- Code of Conduct acknowledgment
- Security Policy acknowledgment
- Equipment receipt form

## 4. First Week

### 4.1 Training Requirements

New employees must complete the following training during their first week:
- Security Awareness Training (2 hours)
- Anti-Harassment Training (1 hour)
- Data Privacy Training (1 hour)
- Company Culture and Values overview (1 hour)
- Role-specific tool training (varies)

### 4.2 Key Meetings

- Meet with each team member individually (30 minutes each)
- Attend first team standup/meeting
- Meet with key cross-functional stakeholders
- Schedule recurring 1:1s with manager (weekly for first 90 days)

## 5. First 30 Days

### 5.1 Goals

- Complete all mandatory training modules
- Understand team workflows and processes
- Complete first small project or deliverable
- Attend at least one company social event
- Provide feedback on onboarding experience via survey

### 5.2 Manager Check-In

At the 30-day mark, managers must conduct a formal check-in to:
- Review progress against the 30-60-90 day plan
- Address any concerns or questions
- Adjust goals if needed
- Discuss initial observations and feedback

## 6. First 60 Days

### 6.1 Goals

- Take ownership of regular responsibilities
- Contribute independently to team projects
- Begin cross-team collaboration
- Identify areas for learning and development

### 6.2 Manager Check-In

The 60-day check-in focuses on:
- Progress toward full productivity
- Integration with team culture
- Any additional support or resources needed
- Preliminary performance feedback

## 7. First 90 Days (End of Probation)

### 7.1 Probationary Review

At the end of 90 days, managers must complete a formal probationary review that includes:
- Assessment of job performance against expectations
- Evaluation of cultural fit and teamwork
- Recommendation: confirm employment, extend probation (up to 30 additional days), or terminate

### 7.2 Confirmation of Employment

Upon successful completion of the probationary period:
- Employee receives confirmation letter
- Full PTO usage becomes available
- Remote work eligibility begins
- Eligible for internal transfers and promotions after 6 months

## 8. Buddy Program

### 8.1 Buddy Responsibilities

- Assist with day-to-day questions and navigation
- Introduce the new hire to colleagues and company culture
- Have lunch or coffee with the new hire at least weekly for the first month
- Provide informal feedback to the manager (with new hire's knowledge)

### 8.2 Buddy Selection

Buddies should be:
- On the same or adjacent team
- Have at least 6 months of tenure
- Be in good performance standing
- Volunteer for the role (not assigned involuntarily)

## 9. Remote Employee Onboarding

Remote employees follow the same onboarding structure with these modifications:
- Equipment is shipped to arrive 2 days before the start date
- Day 1 orientation is conducted via video conference
- Virtual team lunch with delivery meal credit ($30)
- Buddy check-ins conducted via video call daily for the first week
- In-person visit to the office within the first 30 days if possible
