# Business Travel Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Updated:** January 15, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Finance Department

## 1. Purpose

This policy provides guidelines for business travel at Acme Corporation. It ensures employee safety, cost efficiency, and compliance with company standards during business trips.

## 2. Travel Approval

### 2.1 Approval Requirements

| Trip Type | Approval Required |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Domestic, under $1,000 | Manager |
| Domestic, $1,000-$5,000 | Manager + Director |
| Domestic, over $5,000 | Manager + VP |
| International | Manager + VP + Legal |

### 2.2 Advance Booking

All travel must be booked at least 14 days in advance when possible. Last-minute bookings (within 7 days) require director-level approval regardless of cost. Emergency travel (within 48 hours) may be booked with verbal approval and documented retroactively.

### 2.3 Travel Booking Platform

All travel must be booked through the company's travel portal at travel.acmecorp.com. Direct bookings with airlines or hotels are only permitted when the preferred travel portal does not offer the required itinerary.

## 3. Air Travel

### 3.1 Class of Service

- **Flights under 5 hours:** Economy class
- **Flights 5-8 hours:** Premium economy
- **Flights over 8 hours:** Business class (VP approval required)
- **First class:** Not permitted except for medical accommodations

### 3.2 Booking Guidelines

- Book the lowest reasonable fare within a 2-hour window of preferred departure time
- Preferred airlines: United, Delta, American (for loyalty program consolidation)
- Baggage fees for one checked bag are reimbursable
- Seat upgrades purchased with personal funds or miles are not reimbursable

### 3.3 Flight Changes and Cancellations

- Changes due to business needs are covered by the company
- Personal changes to itineraries are the employee's responsibility
- If a flight is cancelled by the airline, rebook immediately and notify your manager

## 4. Ground Transportation

### 4.1 Rental Cars

- Mid-size or smaller vehicles unless traveling with 3+ colleagues
- Always purchase the rental company's collision damage waiver (CDW)
- Refuel before returning the vehicle (fuel charges from rental companies are not reimbursable)
- GPS rentals are not reimbursable (use your phone)
- Toll charges for business routes are reimbursable

### 4.2 Personal Vehicle Use

- Reimbursed at the current IRS rate ($0.655/mile in 2025)
- Mileage logs must be submitted with expense reports
- Company is not liable for personal vehicle damage during business travel
- If driving exceeds 250 miles one-way, a rental car or flight should be considered instead

### 4.3 Ride-Share and Taxi

- Ride-share (Uber, Lyft) preferred over taxis
- Pool/shared rides encouraged when time permits
- Tips up to 20% are reimbursable
- Luxury vehicle options (Uber Black, etc.) are not reimbursable unless traveling with clients

## 5. Lodging

### 5.1 Hotel Guidelines

- Maximum nightly rate: $200 domestic, $300 international
- Preferred hotel chains: Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt
- Book standard rooms; suites require VP approval
- Room service is limited to $30/meal when no reasonable restaurant alternative exists

### 5.2 Extended Stays

For trips exceeding 5 nights, employees should consider:
- Extended-stay hotels (typically more cost-effective)
- Corporate apartment rentals for stays over 2 weeks
- Contact the travel team for assistance with extended stay arrangements

### 5.3 Accommodation Sharing

Employees are never required to share hotel rooms. If employees voluntarily share, each receives a $50/night incentive.

## 6. Meals During Travel

### 6.1 Per Diem Option

Travelers may opt for a per diem instead of itemized meal reimbursement:
- Domestic travel: $75/day
- International travel: $100/day
- Per diem covers all meals and incidental expenses
- No receipts required for per diem

### 6.2 Itemized Reimbursement

If not using per diem, individual meal limits apply:
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $30
- Dinner: $50
- Alcohol: up to $25/day

## 7. Travel Safety

### 7.1 Travel Insurance

The company provides business travel insurance covering:
- Medical emergencies abroad
- Trip cancellation and interruption
- Lost luggage (above airline coverage)
- Emergency evacuation

### 7.2 High-Risk Destinations

Travel to countries with Level 3 or 4 travel advisories requires:
- VP and Legal approval
- Pre-travel safety briefing
- Enrollment in the embassy notification program
- Check-in with HR daily during the trip

### 7.3 Health Precautions

- Required vaccinations for international travel are covered by the company
- Employees with pre-existing medical conditions should consult their doctor before international travel
- Prescription medications should be carried in original containers with documentation

## 8. Combining Personal and Business Travel

Employees may extend business trips for personal travel under these conditions:
- Personal days must be taken as PTO
- The company will cover airfare only up to the cost of the original business itinerary
- Hotel costs during personal days are the employee's responsibility
- Extended travel must be approved by the manager before booking
- Travel insurance covers only the business portion of the trip

## 9. Expense Reporting After Travel

All travel expenses must be submitted within 14 days of trip completion. Receipts are required for all individual expenses over $25. The expense report should include a brief business purpose for the trip. See the Expense Reimbursement Policy for detailed submission guidelines.

## 10. Contact

For travel bookings, contact the travel team at travel@acmecorp.com or ext. 3250. For travel policy questions, contact Finance at expenses@acmecorp.com.
