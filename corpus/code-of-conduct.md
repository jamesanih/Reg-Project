# Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Updated:** January 10, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Legal Department and Human Resources

## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical and behavioral standards expected of all Acme Corporation employees. Our reputation depends on the integrity and professionalism of every team member.

## 2. Scope

This Code applies to all employees, officers, directors, contractors, and interns of Acme Corporation in all work-related settings, including offices, remote work environments, company events, and online communications.

## 3. Core Values

### 3.1 Integrity

Employees must conduct business honestly and ethically. Falsifying records, lying to colleagues or customers, or misrepresenting the company is strictly prohibited. When in doubt about the ethical course of action, consult your manager or the Ethics Hotline.

### 3.2 Respect

Every employee deserves to be treated with dignity and respect. We value diverse perspectives and maintain a welcoming environment for all. Disrespectful behavior including bullying, intimidation, or condescension is not tolerated.

### 3.3 Accountability

Employees are expected to take ownership of their work, meet their commitments, and acknowledge mistakes. Blaming others or concealing errors undermines trust and team performance.

## 4. Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination

### 4.1 Zero Tolerance

Acme Corporation has zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or retaliation based on race, color, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic.

### 4.2 Examples of Prohibited Conduct

- Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favors
- Offensive jokes, slurs, or stereotyping
- Intimidation, threats, or bullying
- Displaying offensive materials in the workplace or virtual backgrounds
- Exclusion from work activities based on protected characteristics

### 4.3 Reporting

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it through any of these channels:
- Direct manager (if not involved in the incident)
- HR department at hr@acmecorp.com
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC (anonymous)
- VP of People Operations

All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### 5.1 Definition

A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests could interfere with or appear to interfere with the company's interests.

### 5.2 Examples

- Having a financial interest in a competitor, supplier, or customer
- Hiring or supervising a family member or romantic partner
- Accepting gifts or entertainment that could influence business decisions
- Engaging in outside employment that conflicts with Acme duties

### 5.3 Disclosure

Employees must promptly disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their manager and the Legal department. Disclosed conflicts will be evaluated and managed on a case-by-case basis.

## 6. Gifts and Entertainment

Employees may accept gifts of nominal value (under $75) as a business courtesy. Gifts exceeding $75 must be reported to the Legal department. Cash gifts of any amount are never acceptable. Government officials may not be given gifts of any value.

## 7. Confidentiality

Employees must protect confidential company information during and after employment. This includes but is not limited to:
- Trade secrets and intellectual property
- Customer and vendor information
- Financial data and projections
- Strategic plans and product roadmaps
- Employee personal information

Confidential information should only be shared on a need-to-know basis and in compliance with the Information Security Policy.

## 8. Social Media

### 8.1 Personal Social Media

Employees may use personal social media but must:
- Clearly state that views are their own, not the company's
- Not disclose confidential company information
- Not disparage the company, colleagues, customers, or partners
- Not use company logos or trademarks without Marketing approval

### 8.2 Official Company Accounts

Only authorized Communications team members may post on official company social media accounts. Content must be approved by the Marketing department before posting.

## 9. Substance Abuse

Acme Corporation maintains a drug-free workplace. Employees may not:
- Use, possess, or distribute illegal drugs on company premises or during work hours
- Be under the influence of drugs or alcohol while performing work duties
- Use prescription medications in a way that impairs work performance

Moderate alcohol consumption is permitted at company-sponsored social events. The company offers confidential employee assistance programs (EAP) for those seeking help with substance abuse.

## 10. Disciplinary Process

Violations of this Code of Conduct will be addressed through a progressive disciplinary process:
1. **Verbal warning** with documented discussion
2. **Written warning** placed in personnel file
3. **Final written warning** or suspension
4. **Termination**

Severe violations (theft, violence, harassment, data breach) may result in immediate termination without progressive steps.

## 11. Acknowledgment

All employees must sign an acknowledgment of this Code of Conduct upon hire and annually thereafter. Acknowledgment forms are processed through the HR portal.

## 12. Contact

For ethics-related questions, contact the Legal department at legal@acmecorp.com or the Ethics Hotline at 1-800-555-ETHIC.
