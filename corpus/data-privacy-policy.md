# Data Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Updated:** January 30, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Legal Department and Information Security Team

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how Acme Corporation collects, processes, stores, and protects personal data. We are committed to complying with all applicable data privacy regulations including GDPR, CCPA, and other relevant laws.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all personal data processed by Acme Corporation, including data belonging to:
- Employees and job applicants
- Customers and prospective customers
- Vendors and partners
- Website visitors

## 3. Definitions

- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual (name, email, phone number, IP address, etc.)
- **Sensitive Personal Data:** Data requiring additional protection (health information, financial data, biometric data, racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs)
- **Data Subject:** The individual whose personal data is being processed
- **Data Controller:** Acme Corporation, which determines how and why personal data is processed
- **Data Processor:** Any third party that processes personal data on behalf of Acme Corporation

## 4. Data Collection Principles

### 4.1 Lawful Basis

Personal data must only be collected when there is a lawful basis:
- Consent from the data subject
- Contractual necessity
- Legal obligation
- Legitimate business interest
- Vital interest of the data subject

### 4.2 Data Minimization

Collect only the minimum personal data necessary for the stated purpose. Do not collect data "just in case" it might be useful later. Review data collection forms annually to ensure they remain necessary.

### 4.3 Purpose Limitation

Personal data collected for one purpose must not be used for a materially different purpose without additional consent or legal basis. Document the purpose for each data collection activity.

## 5. Data Subject Rights

### 5.1 Right of Access

Individuals have the right to request a copy of their personal data. Requests must be fulfilled within 30 days. Submit data access requests to privacy@acmecorp.com.

### 5.2 Right to Rectification

Individuals may request corrections to inaccurate personal data. Corrections must be made within 15 business days.

### 5.3 Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

Individuals may request deletion of their personal data when:
- Data is no longer necessary for its original purpose
- Consent has been withdrawn
- Data was processed unlawfully

Note: This right does not apply when data retention is required by law.

### 5.4 Right to Data Portability

Individuals may request their data in a structured, commonly used format (CSV or JSON). Portability requests must be fulfilled within 30 days.

### 5.5 Right to Object

Individuals may object to processing based on legitimate interest or for direct marketing purposes. Marketing opt-outs must be processed within 5 business days.

## 6. Data Storage and Retention

### 6.1 Storage Requirements

- Personal data must be stored in approved company systems only
- All personal data must be encrypted at rest (AES-256 minimum)
- Access to personal data is restricted on a need-to-know basis
- Personal data must not be stored on personal devices, USB drives, or personal cloud accounts

### 6.2 Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Employee records | 7 years after separation |
| Job applicant data | 2 years after decision |
| Customer data | Duration of relationship + 5 years |
| Financial/tax records | 7 years |
| Website analytics | 26 months |
| Marketing consent records | Duration of consent + 3 years |

### 6.3 Data Disposal

When retention periods expire, data must be securely destroyed:
- Electronic data: Cryptographic erasure or secure overwriting
- Physical documents: Cross-cut shredding
- Storage media: Degaussing or physical destruction

## 7. Data Sharing and Transfer

### 7.1 Internal Sharing

Personal data may be shared internally only with employees who have a legitimate business need. Access requests must be approved by the data owner and documented.

### 7.2 Third-Party Sharing

Before sharing personal data with third parties:
- Execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Verify the third party's security practices
- Ensure the transfer complies with applicable laws
- Document the transfer and its purpose

### 7.3 International Transfers

Transferring personal data outside the country of origin requires:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or equivalent legal mechanism
- Review and approval by the Legal department
- Documentation in the data processing register

## 8. Data Breach Response

### 8.1 Notification Requirements

In the event of a personal data breach:
- The Security team must be notified within 1 hour of discovery
- Affected regulatory authorities must be notified within 72 hours (where required by law)
- Affected individuals must be notified "without undue delay" if the breach poses a high risk to their rights

### 8.2 Breach Response Steps

1. Contain the breach and prevent further data loss
2. Assess the scope and severity
3. Notify the Security and Legal teams
4. Document the breach in the incident register
5. Notify regulators and affected individuals as required
6. Conduct a post-incident review

## 9. Privacy Impact Assessments

A Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) must be conducted before:
- Launching a new product or service that processes personal data
- Implementing new technology that collects personal data
- Changing how personal data is processed in a material way
- Sharing personal data with new third parties

PIAs must be reviewed and approved by the Privacy Officer and Legal.

## 10. Employee Responsibilities

All employees must:
- Complete annual data privacy training
- Report suspected data breaches immediately
- Not access personal data without a business need
- Not share personal data via unsecured channels (personal email, messaging apps)
- Follow data handling procedures outlined in this policy and the Security Policy

## 11. Contact

For data privacy questions, contact the Privacy Officer at privacy@acmecorp.com. For data subject requests, submit a form at privacy.acmecorp.com/request.
