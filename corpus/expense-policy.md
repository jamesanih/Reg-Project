# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Updated:** February 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Finance Department

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the guidelines for business expense reimbursement at Acme Corporation. Employees are expected to exercise good judgment and spend company funds as if they were their own.

## 2. General Guidelines

All business expenses must be:
- Reasonable and necessary for conducting company business
- Pre-approved by your manager for amounts exceeding $100
- Submitted within 30 days of being incurred
- Accompanied by itemized receipts (credit card statements alone are not sufficient)

## 3. Expense Categories and Limits

### 3.1 Meals and Entertainment

| Category | Per-Person Limit | Approval Required |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Individual meal (business travel) | $50/meal | No |
| Team lunch/dinner (local) | $40/person | Manager |
| Client entertainment | $100/person | Director |
| Team building events | $75/person | Manager + HR |

Alcohol may be included in meal expenses up to $25 per person. Tips should not exceed 20%.

### 3.2 Travel

- **Airfare:** Economy class for flights under 5 hours; premium economy permitted for flights over 5 hours. Book at least 14 days in advance when possible.
- **Hotels:** Up to $200/night for domestic travel, up to $300/night for international travel. Use preferred hotel partners listed on the intranet.
- **Rental cars:** Mid-size or smaller vehicles. Always purchase the rental company's insurance.
- **Mileage:** $0.655/mile for personal vehicle use for business purposes.
- **Ride-share/Taxi:** Permitted for airport transfers and local business travel. Prefer ride-share over taxis when available.

### 3.3 Office Supplies and Software

- Office supplies under $50 do not require pre-approval.
- Software subscriptions under $25/month may be expensed with manager approval.
- Software subscriptions over $25/month require IT and manager approval.

### 3.4 Professional Development

- Conference registration: Up to $2,000 per event with manager and director approval.
- Books and online courses: Up to $500 per year with manager approval.
- Professional certifications: Fully covered with manager and HR approval.

## 4. Submission Process

### 4.1 How to Submit

1. Log into the expense portal at expenses.acmecorp.com
2. Create a new expense report grouped by trip or month
3. Upload itemized receipts (photos acceptable)
4. Add business justification for each expense
5. Submit to your manager for approval

### 4.2 Reimbursement Timeline

- Approved expenses are reimbursed within 10 business days via direct deposit.
- Expenses submitted more than 60 days late require VP approval and may be denied.
- Reimbursement is processed on the 1st and 15th of each month.

## 5. Corporate Credit Cards

### 5.1 Eligibility

Employees at the Manager level and above, or those who travel frequently (more than 4 times per year), may request a corporate credit card.

### 5.2 Usage

Corporate credit cards are for business expenses only. Personal charges on corporate cards must be repaid within 5 business days. Monthly reconciliation of corporate card statements is mandatory.

## 6. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are NOT reimbursable:
- Personal entertainment or meals not related to business
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, or towing charges
- Airline upgrades beyond policy (first class, business class on short flights)
- Personal travel insurance
- Gym memberships (unless part of a wellness program)
- Clothing (unless required safety equipment)
- Political or charitable donations
- Expenses for family members or non-employees (unless pre-approved for client events)

## 7. Expense Audits

The Finance department conducts random audits of expense reports quarterly. Employees found submitting fraudulent or inflated expenses will face disciplinary action up to and including termination. Repeated policy violations will result in loss of corporate credit card privileges.
